# Prompt 3: Video Testimonial Script Guide

## Purpose
Generate a complete framework for capturing video testimonials from customers. Includes the pre-call invite email, structured interview questions, recording instructions for the customer, and a story arc guide so the testimonial flows naturally and converts.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a video marketing strategist and customer success expert. Generate a complete video testimonial capture kit for a business.

BUSINESS INFO:
- Business name: [BUSINESS NAME]
- Product/service: [DESCRIPTION]
- Customer segment: [Who is this customer? E.g., "freelance graphic designer", "HR director at a 200-person company", "real estate agent"]
- Key outcome achieved: [Specific measurable result. E.g., "reduced client onboarding time from 3 weeks to 4 days", "landed their first $10K client"]
- Customer name (optional): [FIRST NAME or "a customer"]
- Planned use: [Website / LinkedIn / YouTube / Sales calls / Email campaigns / All of the above]
- Video length target: [Under 60 seconds / 1-2 minutes / 3-5 minutes]
- Recording method: [Zoom call (we record) / Loom (they self-record) / In-person / Phone audio only]

GENERATE ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:

---

SECTION 1: PRE-CALL INVITE EMAIL
Subject line + email body to invite the customer to record a testimonial.
- Position it as an honor, not a burden
- Explain exactly what to expect (time, format, topic)
- Reassure: no preparation needed, casual is fine
- Under 150 words

---

SECTION 2: RECORDING INSTRUCTIONS (for self-recorded Loom/Zoom)
A short, friendly guide to send the customer before they record.
Cover:
- Environment (lighting, background, quiet space)
- Camera/phone setup (eye level, landscape)
- What to wear / what to avoid
- "Mess-ups are fine — just keep going" reassurance
- File delivery instructions
Under 200 words. Bullet point format.

---

SECTION 3: INTERVIEW QUESTIONS (7 questions)
Structured for a natural story arc: Before → Turning Point → After → Would Recommend.
Format each question as:
- The question to ask out loud
- Why this question (what it reveals to a future buyer)
- Coaching tip (how to draw out a better answer if they're vague)

Questions must cover:
1. The situation before they found [BUSINESS NAME]
2. What they tried before that didn't work
3. What made them decide to try [BUSINESS NAME]
4. What surprised them most
5. The specific result they're most proud of
6. How their life/business/work is different now
7. Who they would recommend this to and why

---

SECTION 4: STORY ARC FRAMEWORK
A simple guide for how to edit or sequence the testimonial footage:
- Opening hook (which answer becomes the first 5 seconds)
- Problem statement (60-90 seconds in)
- Result moment (the emotional peak — usually Question 5 or 6)
- Closing recommendation
- Lower-third text suggestions (customer name, company, outcome stat)
- Call-to-action text for video end card

---

REQUIREMENTS:
- Questions must NOT be leading ("You loved it, right?") — open-ended only
- Include one "silence technique" tip: when to pause after an answer to get the customer to elaborate
- The story arc must work for both long-form (3-5 min) and a 60-second cut
```

---

## How to Use

1. Fill in the business info
2. Run in Claude — get all 4 sections
3. Send Section 1 (email) to invite the customer
4. Send Section 2 (recording guide) once they say yes
5. Use Section 3 (questions) during the call or have them respond on Loom
6. Use Section 4 to brief your editor (or edit yourself)

---

## Where to Use Video Testimonials

- **Website homepage**: Hero section social proof (highest ROI placement)
- **Sales calls**: Share screen, play 60-second clip when prospects ask "do you have examples?"
- **LinkedIn**: Native video posts get 3x more reach than text posts
- **Email campaigns**: Embed thumbnail → links to YouTube (auto-increases CTR 200-300%)
- **Paid ads**: Facebook/Instagram video testimonials typically outperform studio creative
