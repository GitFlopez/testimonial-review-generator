# Prompt 4: Social Proof Repurposing Kit

## Purpose
Turn a single customer testimonial into a full suite of marketing assets: 5 platform-native social posts, pull quote card copy, website hero section copy, and a FAQ-style objection handler that embeds the testimonial into the sales conversation.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a conversion copywriter specializing in social proof and testimonial marketing. Take a raw customer testimonial and turn it into a complete multi-platform marketing asset kit.

INPUT:
- Raw testimonial text: [PASTE THE FULL TESTIMONIAL HERE]
- Customer name: [FIRST NAME LAST NAME or "Anonymous"]
- Customer role/title: [e.g., "Freelance Designer", "VP of Marketing at Acme Corp", "Etsy seller with 400+ sales"]
- Business name: [YOUR BUSINESS NAME]
- Product/service being reviewed: [PRODUCT/SERVICE NAME]
- Key result mentioned (extract from testimonial or add): [e.g., "saved 8 hours/week", "increased revenue 40%", "launched in 3 days"]
- Brand voice: [Professional / Conversational / Bold / Minimal]

GENERATE ALL OF THE FOLLOWING:

---

SECTION 1: PLATFORM-NATIVE SOCIAL POSTS (5 posts)

Each post must feel native to the platform — not copy-pasted.

POST 1 — LinkedIn (600-900 characters)
- Open with the result, not "A customer said..."
- Tell the story arc: problem → solution → outcome
- Tag the customer if appropriate (note: "Tag @[CustomerName] if they're on LinkedIn")
- End with a question to drive comments or a soft CTA
- Include 3-5 relevant hashtags

POST 2 — Twitter/X (under 280 characters)
- Lead with the most shocking or specific stat from the testimonial
- Use the testimonial as a pull quote in quotation marks if under 200 chars
- Option: thread structure if the story needs more room (Tweet 1 of 3...)
- 2-3 hashtags max

POST 3 — Instagram (150-220 characters + hashtags)
- Emotion-first opening (the feeling the customer had, not the feature)
- Short. Punchy. Conversational.
- Hashtag block (20-25 tags): mix of broad + niche + branded

POST 4 — Facebook (100-200 characters)
- Community framing ("One of our clients just...")
- Invite engagement ("Has anyone else experienced this?")
- Link-friendly (Facebook suppresses posts with external links — note where to put the link in first comment)

POST 5 — Threads (under 500 characters)
- Conversational, raw, unpolished
- First-person observation framing ("I've been saying this for a while — [testimonial result] is possible")
- End with a question or provocative statement

---

SECTION 2: PULL QUOTE CARD COPY
For graphic design / Canva testimonial cards.

Generate 3 pull quote versions:
- Version A: Full quote (exact words, under 50 words — trim if needed)
- Version B: Power phrase (extract the 8-12 most powerful words from the testimonial)
- Version C: Reframed as a headline ("How [Customer Name] [achieved outcome] with [Business Name]")

For each, include:
- Attribution line: "[Name], [Title], [Company or Segment]"
- Optional subline: key metric or result in bold

---

SECTION 3: WEBSITE HERO SECTION COPY
One complete website hero section built around this testimonial as social proof.

Format:
- H1 headline (benefit-focused, under 10 words)
- H2 subheadline (expands on H1, under 20 words)
- Body paragraph (2-3 sentences, what the product does and for whom)
- Testimonial block (pull quote + attribution)
- Primary CTA button
- Secondary CTA (softer option)

---

SECTION 4: FAQ OBJECTION HANDLER
Turn the testimonial into a natural answer to the most common sales objection it addresses.

Identify which objection this testimonial best handles:
- "Is it worth the price?"
- "Will it work for someone like me?"
- "How fast will I see results?"
- "Is it too complicated?"
- "I've tried similar things before that didn't work"

Then generate:
- The FAQ question (how a prospect would phrase the objection)
- The FAQ answer (2-3 sentences that naturally weave in the testimonial as evidence)
- Where to place this FAQ: [Sales page / Pricing page / Checkout page / Cold email P.S. / Sales call script]

---

REQUIREMENTS:
- Never fabricate metrics not in the original testimonial
- If the testimonial is vague, note where to [INSERT SPECIFIC METRIC] rather than inventing one
- Each social post must be independently usable — no "link in bio" without context
- The hero section copy must work without the testimonial block removed (testimonial is an enhancement, not a crutch)
```

---

## How to Use

1. Collect 1 strong testimonial (written, video transcript, or customer email)
2. Fill in all fields
3. Run in Claude — get all 4 sections
4. Schedule the 5 social posts across 2-3 weeks (not all at once)
5. Upload pull quote card versions to Canva, add customer photo if available
6. Update website hero section with new copy
7. Add FAQ to your pricing page or sales email

---

## Testimonial Collection Tips

- **Best source:** Customer success call recordings (Otter.ai transcripts work perfectly)
- **Second best:** Positive reply to your Day 0 review request email
- **Third best:** Positive DM or tweet — ask permission to use it, then run this prompt
- **Quality filter:** A good testimonial has a Before + After + Specific Result. Vague praise ("great product!") needs coaching — use Prompt 3 to get a better one.
