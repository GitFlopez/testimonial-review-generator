# Prompt 1: Email Review Request Sequence

## Purpose
Generate a 3-email drip sequence to request customer reviews on any platform (Google, Trustpilot, G2, Yelp, App Store, Amazon). Optimized for open rates, click-through, and platform-specific framing.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are an expert in customer success and review acquisition. Generate a 3-email review request sequence for a business.

BUSINESS INFO:
- Business name: [BUSINESS NAME]
- Product/service: [PRODUCT OR SERVICE DESCRIPTION]
- Target review platform: [Google Business / Trustpilot / G2 / Capterra / Yelp / App Store / Amazon / other]
- Review link URL: [DIRECT REVIEW LINK]
- Customer win or outcome: [WHAT DID THE CUSTOMER ACHIEVE? E.g., "cut their monthly expenses by 30%", "launched their first online course in 2 weeks"]
- Sender name: [FIRST NAME OF PERSON SENDING THE EMAIL]
- Tone: [Professional / Casual / Friendly]

SEQUENCE RULES:
- Email 1 (Day 0): Send immediately after a clear customer win or positive interaction. Warm, grateful, low-friction. One click to review.
- Email 2 (Day 3): Follow-up only if no review left. Acknowledge they're busy. Add social proof of why reviews matter. Shorter than Email 1.
- Email 3 (Day 7): Final ask. Personal, slightly more direct. "I won't bug you again." Option to reply with feedback instead if they prefer private.

FORMAT FOR EACH EMAIL:
Subject line: [subject]
Preview text: [preview]

Body:
[email body — plain text format, no HTML, conversational tone]

CTA button text: [button label]
CTA URL: [REVIEW LINK]

REQUIREMENTS:
- Never beg or pressure
- Acknowledge the specific outcome the customer achieved
- Each email under 150 words
- Mobile-optimized (short paragraphs, single CTA)
- Platform-specific framing: for G2/Capterra mention "helps other [job title] find the right tool", for Google mention "helps local people find us", for App Store mention "takes 30 seconds"
```

---

## How to Use

1. Copy the prompt above
2. Fill in all bracketed fields
3. Paste into Claude
4. Copy the 3 emails into your email platform
5. Set up the sequence: Email 1 → wait 3 days → Email 2 (if no click) → wait 4 days → Email 3 (if no click)

---

## Pro Tips

- Trigger Email 1 at the moment of highest satisfaction (right after delivery, after onboarding call, after hitting a milestone)
- For SaaS: trigger when user hits first "aha moment" in-app (not at signup)
- For e-commerce: trigger 7-10 days after delivery (product has been used)
- Personalize the "customer win" field — generic = low conversion, specific = high conversion
- Always use a direct review link (not the homepage) — every extra click = 30% drop-off
